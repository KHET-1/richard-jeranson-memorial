In Loving Memory

Richard Jeranson

Naval Reserve veteran · pattern-maker · musician · inventor · father · grandfather

❖

October 25, 1927 — December 9, 2025 · ninety-eight years

A Life Remembered

His story so far

This is what we know right now — pieced together from family memory. Names, dates, and details will be filled in and corrected as the family gathers more. If you spot something wrong or want to add to it, please tell us.

Beginnings

Born October 25, 1927 in Duluth, Minnesota, in a hospital. Adopted as a child by Ernst Jeranson, who gave him his name and whose memory carries forward through his children and grandchildren.

The Navy years

Enlisted in the United States Naval Reserve as a young man and served as a Petty Officer Third Class, Class V‑6 (USNR). Honorably discharged in 1946 from Naval Air Station Corpus Christi, Texas — the largest naval aviation training command of the Second World War. He later served again during the Korean War.

The pattern shop

Owned and operated a wood and pattern shop in the Minneapolis-St. Paul area for many years. Pattern‑making is the craft behind every casting in industrial manufacturing — the wooden masters foundries pour molten metal around. Quiet work, exact work, the kind that rewarded his hands.

Music

He was a musician as well as a maker, and he didn’t just play guitars — he modified them. The same hands that turned wooden patterns turned them toward instruments. The family is gathering details on the pieces he played and the modifications he was proud of.

Inventor

He held several patents. One the family remembers is a hand-pedaled bicycle — a bike you could power with your arms instead of your legs. There were others. We’re still tracking them down.

Family home

For decades the family home was at 6911 East River Road NE, Fridley, Minnesota — on the Mississippi, looking toward the river all year round.

Mercy Hospital

Had heart surgery at Mercy Hospital in his late 70s and recovered to keep going for almost two more decades. His grandson Ryan was born at the same Mercy Hospital in April 1998 — one Jeranson generation arriving as another was being patched up.

Going home

Passed away December 9, 2025 in Prescott, Arizona, age 98. He’ll be laid to rest with full military honors at Fort Snelling National Cemetery in Minneapolis on May 20, 2026, with a 21‑gun salute — coming home to Minnesota one more time.

His family

His four children are Gary Jeranson, Rick Jeranson (passed), Joan “Joni” Jeranson, and Judy Lions — Joan and Judy are twin sisters. Joan holds three degrees and serves as treasurer at the community center. His grandson Ryan — son of Joan and Robert — is the one gathering this page for the family.

Close friends of the family

Three names belong here as people who walked alongside Richard and the family for the long stretch: Bernice Blaske, June Parks, and Kenny Anderson. Joan thought of June as a mom; both Bernice and June were kind and good people. All three have passed. Their friendship with Richard ran deep and the family carries them.
